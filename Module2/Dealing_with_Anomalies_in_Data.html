Dealing with Anomalies in Data

Introduction

Imagine you're looking at a picture of a beautiful garden. Most of the flowers are blue, but there's one bright red flower in the middle. That red flower stands out, right? In the world of data, this "stand-out" point is called an anomaly. When we look at numbers and data, these anomalies can tell us something interesting or hint that something might be wrong.

Types of Anomalies

There are different ways something can stand out in a dataset:

  • Point anomalies: Like finding a single red apple in a basket of green ones. It's just one odd data point.
  • Contextual anomalies: Imagine you wear a heavy jacket in July. It's weird because July is usually hot, but wearing that jacket in December would be normal. So, the context matters!
  • Collective anomalies: Think of a team where everyone is great individually, but they just can't win when they play together. Each player is fine by themselves, but as a group, something's off.

Handling Anomalies

When we spot these stand-out points, we can't just ignore them. We need to figure out why they're there. Sometimes they're just a fluke, like a rare rainbow-colored butterfly. But other times, they might mean there's a problem, like a leaky faucet causing a water puddle. Deciding what to do depends on what we're looking at and why we think the anomaly happened.
